Buying Guide

  • A property whose price rises and falls repeatedly.
  • If the phone customer service representative and the vehicle service representative are different (they naturally make various excuses)
  • Don't forget that there are no used cars in the world that are cheap and good, and that they show or recommend a different vehicle than the one you consulted about.
  • Use the vehicle registration book or title search to check for vehicle seizures and liens.
  • Used car dealerships sometimes have liens and mortgages attached to them.
  • Even if the seller has completed payment and can simply cancel, you must cancel and proceed with the transaction before transferring ownership and paying the price to ensure a safe transaction.
  • Receive an original copy of the performance/condition inspection record with the signatures and stamps of the inspector and the notifier.
  • If the person responsible for warranty repair is not specified, the person responsible for warranty repair is specified in the contract.
  • Before proceeding with the contract, make sure to check whether there are any additional fees incurred other than the vehicle price.
  • You will receive a receipt for all fees.
  • If you are entrusting the vehicle transfer registration process, ask in advance how much the separate transfer registration fee is, and after the transfer registration is complete, check the receipt for all amounts paid other than the fee.
  • Check if you have a manual. (For imported cars, separate manuals are difficult to obtain and expensive.)
  • Make sure you have a spare key (keys with immobilizer functions or smart keys cost extra).
  • Check the air conditioning in winter and the heater in summer. (Sellers often forget to check this.)
  • Remove the spare tire and check it. (It often contains a tire that is not usable.)
  • Check the availability of vehicle tools and emergency triangle mounts (items that are difficult to request after delivery).
  • Before shipping, the underbody of the vehicle is inspected for corrosion and impact.
  • Verify that the GPS and mechanical system are functioning properly by moving the navigation-equipped vehicle.
  • Check the remaining warranty period and any coupons provided when the new car was delivered. (Electronic coupons may not be visible.)
